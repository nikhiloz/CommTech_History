# Ancient Communication Timeline

## Era Overview Flow
```text
  [3500 BC: Writing] -> [3000 BC: Hieroglyphs] -> [500 BC: Royal Road] -> [150 BC: Polybius Square]
```

- **Prehistory:** Oral traditions and cave paintings used to convey stories and knowledge.
- **c. 3500 BC:** Cuneiform script developed in Mesopotamia, one of the earliest systems of writing.
- **c. 3000 BC:** Hieroglyphics used in Ancient Egypt for record-keeping and monumental inscriptions.
- **c. 500 BC:** Persian Empire establishes the "Royal Road" with a system of relay stations for swift communication by horse-mounted messengers.
- **c. 150 BC:** Polybius square, a Greek method for mapping letters to numerical coordinates, used for signaling.
