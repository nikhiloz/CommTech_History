# Era: Early Ages — Visual Communication

## 1. Executive Summary
Before electronic networks, visual communication provided the fastest means to span distances, transforming human capability to coordinate actions over space.

## 2. Theoretical Principles
Visual systems rely on **line-of-sight propagation** and **optical encoding**. They are fundamentally limited by the **inverse-square law** (for light intensity) and atmospheric conditions (fog, rain).

## 3. The ASCII Representation
```text
  Beacon Site 1      Line of Sight       Beacon Site 2
       |                   |                   |
```

## 4. Historical Context & Societal Impact
Essential for defense and early governance. Empire management relied on these to extend the reach of the central command.

## 5. Technical Limitations & Challenges
Limited bandwidth (only binary signaling), susceptibility to environmental interference, and need for high-visibility infrastructure.

## 6. Key Innovations/Enablers
- **Lighthouse/Tower Networks:** The precursor to modern cellular tower layouts.
- **Telescopic Relays:** Extending range by amplifying human vision.
