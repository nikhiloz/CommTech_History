# Wired Era: Telephony

Telephony brought the immediacy of the human voice to long-distance communication, creating a more intimate and direct method of interaction than telegraphy.

## Key Developments
- **The Voice Medium:** Unlike the symbolic coding of telegraphy, telephony transmitted the analog wave patterns of the human voice.
- **Microphone and Speaker:** Foundational inventions—the carbon microphone for transforming sound waves into electrical signals, and the electromagnetic speaker for reversing the process.
- **Switching Networks:** The evolution from direct point-to-point connections to complex, centralized switching networks (manual, then automatic) that allowed any user to connect to any other user.
- **Infrastructure:** The massive deployment of copper wire networks across urban and rural landscapes.
- **Societal Shift:** Telephony redefined social boundaries, facilitated personal relationships, and revolutionized business and administrative efficiency.
