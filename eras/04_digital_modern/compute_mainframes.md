# Era: Digital — Mainframes

## 1. Executive Summary
Mainframes were the backbone of early enterprise computing, offering massive data processing capabilities to large organizations through centralized, batch-oriented architectures.

## 2. Theoretical Principles
Utilizes **centralized processing** and **multi-user time-sharing** OS architectures. Jobs are submitted via batch processing (queued) or through remote terminals, emphasizing **high availability** and **data integrity** over interactive responsiveness.

## 3. The ASCII Representation
```text
  [Terminals/Clients] --(Comm Lines)--> [Front-End Processor] -> [Central Processor Unit (CPU)] <-> [Memory/Storage]
                                                                        |
                                                                 [Batch Controller]
                                                                        |
                                                                 [Peripheral Hub]
```

## 4. Historical Context & Societal Impact
Enabled the automation of massive institutional tasks (banking, government record-keeping, airline reservations), catalyzing the digitalization of the global economy.

## 5. Technical Limitations & Challenges
Limited interaction speed, massive energy consumption/cooling requirements, high cost of entry, and rigid, proprietary hardware/software stacks.

## 6. Key Innovations/Enablers
- **Time-Sharing OS:** Allowing multiple users to access compute power concurrently.
- **Batch Processing Logic:** Efficient job scheduling for non-interactive workloads.
