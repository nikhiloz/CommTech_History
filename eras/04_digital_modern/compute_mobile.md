# Era: Digital — Mobile Computing

## 1. Executive Summary
Mobile computing represented the culmination of miniaturization, evolving computing from stationary desk-based systems into portable, always-connected digital companions.

## 2. Theoretical Principles
Utilizes **System-on-Chip (SoC)** architectures to integrate CPU, GPU, and I/O into a power-optimized package. Depends on **Always-on connectivity** (Cellular/WiFi) and **Power management** techniques to maintain functionality under strict thermal/battery constraints.

## 4. Historical Context & Societal Impact
Completely transformed individual behavior (instant access to global info, media, and location-based services), enabled the App-based economy, and broke the "desk" dependency.

## 5. Technical Limitations & Challenges
Severe power/thermal constraints, limited screen real estate, reliance on variable radio propagation quality, and complex ecosystem/platform security management.

## 6. Key Innovations/Enablers
- **SoC Integration:** Maximizing performance-per-watt.
- **Advanced Power Management:** Dynamic frequency/voltage scaling.
