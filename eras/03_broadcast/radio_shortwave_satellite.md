# Era: Broadcast — Shortwave and Satellite Radio

## 1. Executive Summary
Shortwave and satellite technologies extended radio communication globally, overcoming the terrestrial limitations of line-of-sight propagation.

## 2. Theoretical Principles
**Shortwave** utilizes **ionospheric refraction** to "bounce" radio waves over the horizon. **Satellite** utilizes **geostationary or LEO relay** transponders to bridge massive distances, bypassing Earth's curvature.

## 4. Historical Context & Societal Impact
Enabled international broadcasting (e.g., Cold War propaganda, international news), maritime safety, and amateur radio global connectivity, breaking down national media monopolies.

## 5. Technical Limitations & Challenges
Shortwave is highly dependent on unpredictable solar activity/ionosphere conditions. Satellite requires complex, expensive orbital infrastructure and directional ground tracking.

## 6. Key Innovations/Enablers
- **Ionospheric Understanding:** Predicting propagation patterns.
- **Satellite Transponders:** High-gain, frequency-shifting repeaters in orbit.
