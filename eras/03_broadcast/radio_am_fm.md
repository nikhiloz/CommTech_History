# Era: Broadcast — Radio (AM/FM)

## 1. Executive Summary
Radio introduced the ability to transmit voice, music, and data wirelessly to a mass audience simultaneously, creating the first true mass-medium.

## 2. Theoretical Principles
Utilizes **electromagnetic waves** as carriers. Information is encoded via **Amplitude Modulation (AM)** (varying wave intensity) or **Frequency Modulation (FM)** (varying wave frequency). The process relies on a modulator to mix the low-frequency audio signal with a high-frequency carrier wave.

## 3. The ASCII Representation
```text
  Audio In (Mic) --> Modulator (AM) --> Amplifier --> Antenna --(RF)--> Antenna --> Receiver (Demod) --> Audio Out (Speaker)
                          |
                    Carrier Freq (f_c)
```

## 4. Historical Context & Societal Impact
Revolutionized political discourse, entertainment culture, and disaster management (public safety alerts). It allowed for real-time information dissemination on a national/global scale.

## 5. Technical Limitations & Challenges
Signal propagation limitations (line-of-sight for FM, ionospheric skip for AM), noise/interference (especially AM), and limited spectrum availability.

## 6. Key Innovations/Enablers
- **Vacuum Tubes:** Essential for signal amplification and oscillation.
- **Heterodyning:** Enabling practical receiver tuning and frequency selection.
